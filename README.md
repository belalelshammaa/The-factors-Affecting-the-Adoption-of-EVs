Electric vs Petrol Vehicle Analysis


This project analyzes the key factors influencing the adoption of Electric Vehicles (EVs) compared to traditional petrol vehicles. 
The study focuses on how economic conditions, charging infrastructure, government subsidies, and fuel prices affect EV market share across different countries and regions.

Using real-world observational data, the project applies statistical analysis, correlation analysis, data visualization, 
and regression modeling to identify patterns and relationships between variables. The findings show that EV adoption is influenced 
by a combination of multiple factors rather than a single variable.

Project Objectives

Analyze the impact of fuel prices on EV adoption.
Study the effect of charging infrastructure availability.
Evaluate the role of government subsidies in increasing EV market share.
Examine how GDP per capita influences EV adoption trends.


Methods Used

Descriptive Statistics
Correlation Heatmap
Scatter Plot Analysis
Regression Modeling
Data Visualization


Key Findings

Higher fuel prices are associated with increased EV adoption.
More charging stations lead to higher EV market share.
Government subsidies encourage EV purchases.
Wealthier regions tend to adopt EVs more rapidly.
